47 /*
48  * We use `std::chrono::duration` for clock types to provide type safety. But unlike the predefined std types
49  * (`std::chrono::milliseconds` et al), CHIP uses unsigned base types, and types are explicity sized, with
50  * smaller-size types available for members and arguments where appropriate.
51  *
52  * Most conversions are handled by the types transparently. To convert with possible loss of information, use
53  * `std::chrono::duration_cast<>()`.
54  */
